Ball Leveler

A PID-controlled system that stabilizes a ball at the center of a platform using IR sensors and an ATmega328P microcontroller. Achieved sub-5mm steady-state error with a custom tuned feedback loop.

As seen from above, the MG966R servo is powered by an external DC source so as to not damage the Arduino output pins. The MG966R draws 2.5A of stall current, which is significantly higher than the 40mA a ATmega328P pin could handle.

The PID controller was tuned iteratively using step response testing. Final gains of Kp=18, Ki=7.5, Kd=0.5 produced stable leveling across a range of initial disturbances. The system was demonstrated at the end-of-semester design expo.
